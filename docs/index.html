04 — Full disclosure

What PoseCap does & doesn't do

Written so you can rule it out quickly if it's wrong for you. These are current product facts, not promises.

◤ What it does

Real-time webcam capture inside Blender
Pose applies to your armature live, without a file round-trip through an external service.
100% local — nothing is uploaded
No account, no cloud, no footage leaving your machine. Relevant if you work on unreleased or client material.
Free and unmetered
Open-source. No per-second billing, no capture cap.
GUI installer, no command line
Built for animators and editors, not developers.

◣ What it does not do (yet)

No cleanup layer
Pose is applied as captured. Several paid tools ship jitter smoothing and foot-locking; PoseCap does not yet, so expect to clean up takes.
Retargeting is SMPL-X-only
No out-of-the-box Mixamo / UE5 / arbitrary-rig retargeting today.
Body only, one person
Face capture is out of scope for now; hands are in progress; multi-person is not supported.
Hardware and platform limits
The accelerated PEAR backend needs an NVIDIA CUDA GPU; a MediaPipe CPU backend widens that. Windows-first.
No published accuracy benchmark
We have not yet published measured accuracy or jitter figures for PoseCap, so we make no quality claim against the tools above.

05 — Know the limits

Every single-camera tool fights the same physics

Useful to know before you judge any of these tools — including this one. These artifacts are documented in peer-reviewed computer-vision literature and acknowledged in vendors' own documentation and release notes.

1
Temporal jitter
Shakiness on limbs that should be still. Multiple vendors ship dedicated smoothing filters — evidence the artifact is real across the field.
2
Foot sliding
Feet drift instead of planting. The most frequently patched artifact in vendor release notes, and usually correctable in cleanup.
3
Occlusion & depth
Crossed limbs, turning away from camera, depth drift. Every vendor's docs advise against occlusion for this reason.
Root cause
One camera → depth ambiguity + self-occlusion
Shows up as
Jitter · foot slide · depth drift · leaning
Published finding
Kinematics-only pipelines can't remove it (PhysCap, SIGGRAPH Asia 2020)
Why it matters
Multi-camera setups exist precisely to escape this ceiling

06 — Show the work

Method, sources & disclaimers

A comparison published by a participant is only worth the audit trail behind it. Here's ours.

▸ How this was researched ›

Three research passes were run using AI agents with web access. Each extracted claim was checked by three independent verifier agents instructed to refute it against primary sources; a claim needed at least two of three to survive. Prices and feature facts were fetched live from official vendor pages on 2026-07-17.

This method has limits. It is automated research, not hands-on benchmarking. No tool here was tested side by side by us on identical footage. Treat this as a well-sourced map of what vendors publish, not as measured performance.

▸ Claims our own fact-check killed ›

Published for transparency — several of these would have made the comparison more flattering to PoseCap. They failed verification, so they are not used above:

“Foot sliding is the #1 complaint.” Refuted. Jitter is at least co-equal and leads ordered lists in sources.
“Move.ai has no free tier.” Refuted as overstated — Move One includes 30 one-time credits (offline, single-camera).
“MocapNET runs real-time on CPU with no GPU at 70 fps.” Refuted; its reported ~30 Hz figure depends on MediaPipe and is a single-machine self-report.
“Cascadeur isn't a motion-capture tool.” Refuted — it does offer offline, file-based video mocap; it simply isn't real-time webcam capture.
“QuickMagic has no Blender path.” Refuted — a Blender/BVH path exists; we did not verify its exact shape.

We also dropped a subjective “output quality ranking” of competitors entirely: it rested on impressions and secondary reviews rather than measurements, so publishing it would have been unfair to the tools ranked low.
